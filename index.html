Preparing for HCMC

Checklist & Essentials

Logistics

  • • Arrive 4 days before start date to recover from jetlag.
  • • Passport: >6 months validity is required.
  • • ITG provides health insurance upon arrival.
  • • Carry 3-month supply of prescription medication.

Hard to Find

  • • Contact Lenses: Expensive in Vietnam.
  • • Tampons: Difficult to buy compared to pads.
  • • Large Shoes: Male >size 10, Female >size 5.
  • • Suncream: Locals often contain whiteners.
  • • British Tea: Bring Yorkshire or PG Tips.

Work Kit

  • • ITG Clothing: Check the Staff Dress Code.
  • • Laptop: Bring your own & charger.
  • • Adapters: Easy to buy locally.

Where to Live?

District 1

The dynamic fusion of old and new. Iconic Notre Dame Cathedral, Ben Thanh Market, and Bui Vien street.

Thao Dien (D2)

HCMC's expatriate bubble! Serene, upscale area with trendy boutiques, international schools and cafes.

District 3

French colonial architecture, tree-lined streets, and iconic attractions like the War Remnants Museum.

Rental Rules to Remember

  • Contract: Must be in writing. Request a bilingual version.
  • Deposit: Usually equivalent to 2 months' rent (VND).
  • Police: Landlord will assist with residency registration.
  • Utilities: Usually paid separately (Electricity, Water, Internet).
  • Lease: One year leases are common.
  • Termination: 1 to 3 months' notice is standard.

Getting Around

Motorbikes & Xe-Ôm

License required for insurance. Shops: Saigon Motorcycles, DC Motorbikes. Always wear an EU standard helmet!

Taxis & Apps

Use Grab, GoJek & Be. Reliable companies: Vinasun & Mai Linh. Costs ~30k (£1) to 150k (£5).
